I know it‚Äôs tempting to find the hottest new niche and spend an afternoon setting up a store. In fact, that‚Äôs what a large number of dropshippers do. They see which niche products are exploding and blindly hop on the bandwagon.

However, this is not a good method. You might achieve runaway success for a while, but after that ends, you‚Äôll probably watch your business fall apart. This happens to almost every entrepreneur who capitalizes on the latest craze. A business model like that simply isn‚Äôt meant to last, and my goal is to help you create a sustainable dropshipping retail career.

If you want to turn dropshipping into a steadily flowing stream of income, you‚Äôll need to choose your niche carefully. In fact, niche selection is one of the most important business decisions you‚Äôll ever make. It sets the stage for everything else to follow.

Think about it. The niche you choose will influence:

  • Which suppliers you work with
  • What marketing strategies you use
  • How successful your business will ultimately be

It‚Äôs a big decision, and it‚Äôs not one to be taken lightly. Too often, dropshippers realize the niche they chose isn‚Äôt working out, and they have to go back square one. That can be a colossal waste of time, money, and other important resources, especially if you‚Äôve been at it for a while.

The more thought you put into selecting a niche, the sturdier your business will be. You won‚Äôt doubt yourself a few months down the road. And that‚Äôs what this chapter is all about: helping you find the best dropshipping niche for your business.

I find that a lot of success with dropshipping comes from asking the right questions. So without further ado, let‚Äôs look at the most important questions you need to ask yourself when selecting a niche.

How to Evaluate Dropshipping Niche Ideas¬∂

Choosing a niche can be confusing. There are millions of niches, so it‚Äôs tricky to know which ones are viable. To make matters worse, many of those niches have established businesses you‚Äôd be competing directly against. It‚Äôs the million dollar question: how do you break into a profitable niche as a newbie?

When you‚Äôre thinking about this, it may seem like every niche is saturated, but that‚Äôs not true. There are niches that still have a genuine need for product purchasing options. Some niches haven‚Äôt been tapped into heavily, while other niches have product-based needs that your store could help with.

However, it can be difficult to know where to just start looking for optimal dropshipping niche ideas. Where do you look? How do you look? What criteria should you use for finding good niches? To the beginner, these are daunting questions.

There‚Äôs a reason it‚Äôs so intimidating: niche evaluation is really a specialized field of its own. You could spend months choosing the best niche, but that‚Äôs not exactly practical for dropshipping. Luckily, there are tactics you can use to choose a great niche in a matter of weeks (or even days, though you shouldn‚Äôt rush it).

Basically, you have to determine if a niche is suitable based on the following:

  • The level of competition
  • The demand for physical niche products

The best dropshipping niche would have low to medium competition and high demand. It would also stay popular indefinitely. Of course, niches like this don‚Äôt exist, so you have to figure out how to balance these criteria. The right balance for you might not be right for another entrepreneur, so you have to think critically about which elements are most important to you.

Here‚Äôs a simple two-step process you can use to start your search for a niche. It revolves around the idea that some niches are better for physical products than others. Once you‚Äôve found those niches, you can ask other questions to help you narrow it down further.

Step 1: Search for trending or low-Competition niches.¬∂

Begin by doing a simple Google search for ‚Äòtrending niches‚Äô or ‚Äòlow-competition niches‚Äô. You‚Äôll have to narrow your search a lot, but this is a basic start. You‚Äôre just taking baby steps for now and getting a very big picture sense of the various dropshipping niche ideas out there.

finding niche products

Typically when you search for trending niches, you‚Äôll find lists of niche products that are currently popular or predicted to become popular in the coming months. These have a lot of potential, but they may also get crowded.

Low-competition niches, by definition, won‚Äôt be too crowded, but this could limit how much you can earn. Niches with super-low levels of competition will usually have smaller audiences, and that translates into a smaller customer base for your business.

In addition, some niches won‚Äôt be right for dropshipping, so you‚Äôll want to make sure you‚Äôre looking at product-based niches. (For example, explainer videos have surged in popularity, but an explainer video is not a physical product, so dropshipping is out of the question.)

When you‚Äôre looking at niches, you want to make sure they meet a few important criteria. You could do this later, but I feel it‚Äôs best to filter your results early on. Here are some questions to ask:

Is This a Product-Based Niche?¬∂

First, it‚Äôs essential to make sure the niche you‚Äôre considering has a decent amount of physical products associated with it. Some niches, like explainer videos, have no product-based needs at all. Other niches do have product-based needs but not many. For example, niches that mostly deal with digital products, like cryptocurrency, aren‚Äôt all that practical for dropshipping because of the low amount of physical products available.

As you do more research, you‚Äôll see that certain niches are inherently more plentiful with products than others. For example, clothing is better than time management for dropshipping because it offers more product variety.

The more niche products you can offer, the better. You don‚Äôt want to sell everything under the sun, but you do want to have a good amount of variety. That‚Äôs why a niche that has lots of products is great for dropshipping. It gives you a ton of room for growth and expansion.

This can be tough because with a high amount of product variety, there‚Äôs often a high level of competition. However, if you look outside of the most popular niches, you should be able to find some niches that aren‚Äôt too competitive yet still have a good amount of product variety.

Last but not least, make sure the products are easily accessible for both you and your customers. For example, custom made items are generally more difficult to dropship, and they won‚Äôt give you the best profit margins. You also want to consider the fact that the more obscure niche products won‚Äôt have a broad appeal. As a general rule, a product that appeals to a wide group of people within the niche is a good product to dropship.

How Popular Is This Niche?¬∂

Ideally, you‚Äôre after a niche that‚Äôs not too popular. You want to choose a niche that has sufficient interest and product demand to sustain a retail business. Often, you will have to compromise, and it‚Äôs a matter of knowing how to compromise.

When conducting niche research, if you can tell right away that a niche is incredibly popular, be warned that it may be difficult to break into. As a new dropshipper, you‚Äôll face the challenge of getting your brand seen, and it‚Äôs much easier in a lower competition niche (even though bigger niches may be tempting and seem easy).

Is This Niche Just the Result of a Passing Fad?¬∂

Every year, there are several handfuls of trends that come and go in a matter of months (sometimes weeks). Businesses pop up left and right to capitalize on these trends, but when the trends die, so do the businesses.

At the same time, a new trend could become the next big niche. Lately, niche products like drones, fidget spinners, and virtual reality gear have become hugely popular in a matter of months, and the dropshippers in these niches have scored big. However, there‚Äôs always a risk involved with going with the coolest new niche, so it is usually safer to choose a niche that‚Äôs been around for a while.

If you find a niche that‚Äôs relatively new, it might not be a good idea to pursue it, but you have to evaluate this on a case-by-case basis. As a rule of thumb, you should give preference to niches with established (or at least devoted) communities.

Am I Too Passionate About This Niche?¬∂

I know this seems like a weird question because usually passion is a good thing. But in this case, too much passion can actually be detrimental to your dropshipping career.

Let‚Äôs say you have a lifelong love of lava lamps. That passion is great, but it won‚Äôt necessarily translate into sales. You can‚Äôt build a dropshipping career on lava lamps because they‚Äôre very niche and not big sellers. You can have all the passion in the world for lava lamps, but you can‚Äôt magically turn them into a sustainable career.

In addition, passion can often blind you to the harsh truths that come with operating a business. Maybe certain niche products aren‚Äôt selling well but you want to keep it around anyway because you like it and believe it will do well. So while it seems like having lots of passion is good, it‚Äôs usually ideal to have a fair amount of detachment from the niche products you choose.

That‚Äôs not to say you can‚Äôt pick your favorite niche for dropshipping. If you‚Äôre able to objectively evaluate your niche and if it looks like a good choice, go for it. Getting an outside view helps a lot with this decision.

You need to step back from what you think of a niche and look at it through an entrepreneurial lens. You‚Äôre going to have to make business decisions based on facts and figures, and you need to be able to do that easily without being too emotionally or personally invested.

Can I Actually Add Value to This Niche?¬∂

This is another question few dropshippers ask. Most people think that opening up a store is a form of adding value, but that‚Äôs not always true.

If you‚Äôre opening a store that‚Äôs basically a carbon copy of an existing store, you‚Äôre not doing anything new or unique. You have very little to differentiate yourself from your competitors.

adding value

But the most problematic thing is that some entrepreneurs who start dropshipping businesses like this don‚Äôt want to provide value. These are the get-rich-quick schemers who see dropshipping as a means to an end. Customers can see right through this, and it‚Äôs also a poor attitude to have in general.

It‚Äôs easy to tell if a business is earnestly trying to provide value or just trying to make a quick buck. People are more likely to support businesses that give them value. By putting your customers first and emphasizing value, you‚Äôll be creating the best conditions for your business to grow.

If you don‚Äôt focus on value, you‚Äôll have a harder time selling.

That‚Äôs why you need to be honest with yourself here. Can you really add value to the niche you‚Äôre considering? Can you offer something different to customers who have a bunch of retailers to choose from?

One of the best ways to evaluate this is by looking at retailers who are already in the niche, and that‚Äôs what the second step is all about. However, this is a question you should start thinking about now.

If you feel like you can‚Äôt take an interesting angle or do something differently, reconsider the niche (as painful as that may be). Rather revise your niche than choose one that won‚Äôt work for you.

Step 2: Search for existing retailers.¬∂

Next, spend some time researching the retailers who are already established in the niche. For now, simple Google searches will suffice. Use various combinations and product keywords to see who‚Äôs selling what, and pay attention to who the big players are (i.e., the retailers you see over and over again).

This step really deserves an analytical approach if you want to get the most out of it. I recommend keeping a list of the retailers you find and making sure to record details like their name, website, and social handles. (A spreadsheet is great for this.)

Don‚Äôt just look at the most popular stores ‚Äì search high and low, and look at everything that‚Äôs out there. There may be smaller retailers who are becoming popular and doing something unique, and analyzing these stores is just as important as analyzing the big names in your niche.

And don‚Äôt be afraid to take extensive notes on what you see. Often it‚Äôs the little things that matter, and scrutinizing details can help you understand why some retailers‚Äô strategies are so successful. Once you understand what they‚Äôre doing, you can break it down and use the knowledge for yourself.

Once you‚Äôve gotten a list of 5-10 retailers, take this opportunity to also look at each store‚Äôs online presence. You don‚Äôt need to do extensive research at this point. Just take a look at each retailer‚Äôs site and social media accounts to get a rough idea of their strategies. See what kinds of content they post, how they engage customers, and when they mention their products.

If you can‚Äôt find many retailers, that could be a good or a bad sign. Low competition is great, but you still need to have a sizeable audience to buy your niche products. This means that if you barely find any retailers, you‚Äôll need to look at pages and online communities dedicated to the niche. Lots of activity is good, but low activity means this niche is probably a bad choice. It could also mean that this niche doesn‚Äôt have a huge need for physical products.

This step doesn‚Äôt need to determine the niche you choose, but it should give you an idea of what you‚Äôre dealing with. It will clue you into how popular the niche is and how much value you could give customers. Again, don‚Äôt be afraid to spend a lot of time on this step.

When you‚Äôre finished, you should have a list of niches that are practical for dropshipping, along with the details of retailers who are already in those niches.

Is It Better to Choose a Super Saturated or Super Unsaturated Niche?¬∂

The perfect niche The perfect niche would have low competition but a large audience. In the real world, that rarely happens. The typical niche is moderately saturated, with a reasonable number of retailers and an audience that‚Äôs not too big or too small.

But what if you‚Äôre stuck between choosing a super-saturated or a super-unsaturated niche? That‚Äôs a tough decision. It‚Äôs important to note that you probably won‚Äôt have to choose between these two extremely different options. Most of the time, you‚Äôll find niches that aren‚Äôt completely saturated or unsaturated.

If those are your only choices, you might want to go back to the drawing board and consider other dropshipping niche ideas. It‚Äôs hard to be profitable in a niche that has a huge amount of competition, but it‚Äôs just as hard to be profitable in a niche that doesn‚Äôt generate a lot of interest.

Like I said, this won‚Äôt happen often, but it‚Äôs important to think about because it makes you calibrate your mindset. You shouldn‚Äôt focus on making profit so much that you skimp on choosing the right niche. Entering a huge dropshipping niche seems like it‚Äôs a good idea, but in reality, you can have a lot of trouble selling niche products when you have thousands and thousands of competitors.

Is the Niche Sustainable?¬∂

This is another major question you need to ask about any niche you‚Äôre seriously considering for dropshipping. Whether you‚Äôre dropshipping on the side or full-time, you need to think about how sustainable a niche will be for you.

Even if you‚Äôve already determined the niche is not just a fad that will blow over in a matter of months, you still need to think about the future. Considering the long term can mean the difference between success and failure. Plenty of niche products seem ideal for dropshipping, but when you think about where those niches will be a year from now, you start to realize that they might not be so promising.

Let‚Äôs go back to the example of phone cases. While the phone case niche is extremely saturated, there will always be a need for phone cases. Now let‚Äôs take another niche product with a very specific angle: eclipse-related products for the United States. When total solar eclipses happen, people go crazy with buying all kinds of gear related to the phenomenon. It seems like it‚Äôd be a no-brainer to set up a dropshipping retailer for this.

unique niche products

However, total solar eclipses don‚Äôt occur in the U.S. very often. Because of that, a dropshipping store that sells eclipse products would only get business during eclipse years. Most years, it would get zero sales, which means these niche products are a poor choice for a long-term dropshipping business.

Finally, let‚Äôs consider a third niche: clothing with catchphrases. This is a tough trend to forecast because some catchphrases stick around while others don‚Äôt. (Think about the lasting ‚ÄúKeep Calm and ____‚Äù trend.)

When thinking about sustainability, ask yourself what the lifespan of the niche is. Do you see this niche being as popular a year from now? What about five years? What do experts think?

This can be a tough nut to crack, and sometimes there will be risk involved because it‚Äôs impossible to predict the future of any given niche. Just be sure you‚Äôre not pouring all your money into a fad that will be over a month or a year from now.

Niche Evaluation in Action¬∂

Now you know all of the strategies for selecting which niche to choose, but maybe you‚Äôre a little confused about how it all comes together. So let‚Äôs put all of this into practice by looking at an example.

Let‚Äôs say you‚Äôre looking to set up your first dropshipping store, and you‚Äôve searched the Internet for some niches that are ripe for new businesses.

To narrow this list down to just one final choice, you can ask the questions listed in the 2-step process above. Here‚Äôs how that might look.

The first step is to filter out the niches that don‚Äôt have a large need for physical products. But even once you‚Äôve done that, you want to gauge how well physical products could perform in the remaining niches.

Right off the bat, you can answer ‚Äòno‚Äô for coding. Coding has a few physical products associated with it, but for the most part it‚Äôs a digital-based niche. So you could remove that from your list.

The other three niches all have specific physical products associated with them, so they get a ‚Äòyes‚Äô for this question.

Now onto part 2 of this question. Exactly how big of a need does each niche have for products? You could argue that all three have fairly large needs, so for now you can keep considering all of them.

Question 2: How popular is the niche?¬∂

Next, it‚Äôs time to examine the popularity of each niche examine the popularity of each niche. Your answers here won‚Äôt necessarily eliminate any niches from your list. Instead, this step is meant to give you a sense of what you‚Äôre dealing with.

The problem with this question is that niche products fluctuate in popularity from time to time, so there are no hard-and-fast answers to this. It‚Äôs a question you have to answer for yourself. This is an easy one. For fidget spinners, you can quickly answer ‚Äòyes‚Äô, so that one has to go. Those niche products are definitely part of a fad that won‚Äôt carry on into the future.

On the other hand, custom T-shirts and travel products are not fads, so they can stay.

This question will often decide whether you eliminate a niche or not. You have to put away your dreams of making it big and be brutally honest with yourself. Will you be able to fill a need that other merchants aren‚Äôt filling? Will you be able to offer something new or different to prospective customers? This is a difficult question to answer, but in this example, the two niches left (custom T-shirts and travel products) are quite different in terms of market saturation. There‚Äôs no question that there are far more custom T-shirt stores than there are travel products stores.

It‚Äôs also difficult to add value to a niche when it‚Äôs so saturated, and you might find that‚Äôs the case with custom T-shirts. Final Thoughts¬∂

Before I conclude this chapter, I want to say a few words on the process of niche selection.

I hope this example process has helped you see how to execute these strategies yourself. It won‚Äôt always be as easy as this to choose a niche, so be prepared to dig in and do some research. When you use this process, be thorough. Even if you narrow down your list to one niche early on, you still need to ask the rest of these questions. Finally, bear in mind that you may need to revisit this step. The next chapter discusses how to select niche products, which is another complex issue in itself. You may find that your ideal niche has a super-crowded product landscape, or that it hasn‚Äôt been profitable for a long time.

In those kinds of situations, you might want to go through the niche selection process all over again. This can seem like grunt work, but you have to push through it until you find a profitable, sustainable niche with a good variety of products.

You‚Äôve Got a Dropshipping Niche! Now What?¬∂

Congratulations! You‚Äôve completed one of the toughest steps in the journey!

After that, you can start the process of choosing the best niche products for your store.
